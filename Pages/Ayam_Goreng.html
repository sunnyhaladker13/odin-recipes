Indonesian fried chicken is a crispy and flavorful delight. Marinated in spices like turmeric, garlic, and coriander,
it resonates with the South Indian love for fried chicken. The distinct Indonesian touch comes from the addition of
ingredients like galangal and kaffir lime leaves, adding layers of aroma.

Recipe

Prep time: 15 min

Cook time: 20 - 25 min

Ingredients

  • For the chicken:
    • 1 chicken leg quarter (or about 1/2 lb bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces)
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • For the Marinade:
    • 1 shallot, roughly chopped
    • 1 clove garlic
    • 1/2-inch piece of ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
    • 1/2-inch piece of galangal (optional, but adds a nice flavor)
    • 1/2-inch piece of turmeric, peeled and roughly chopped (or 1/4 tsp turmeric powder)
    • 1/2 stalk lemongrass, white part only, roughly chopped
    • 1 kaffir lime leaf (optional)
    • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Make the marinade: In a mortar and pestle or a food processor, grind the
    shallot, garlic, ginger, galangal (if using), turmeric, lemongrass, and kaffir lime
    leaf (if using) into a smooth paste. Alternatively, you can finely mince everything
    with a knife. Add the coriander powder, cumin powder, salt, and pepper, and mix
    well.
  2. Marinate the chicken: Place the chicken in a bowl and add the marinade. Rub
    the marinade all over the chicken, ensuring it's well coated. Cover and
    refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably longer (up to overnight) for
    maximum flavor.
  3. Cook the chicken: Heat the oil in a skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add
    the marinated chicken and cook, turning occasionally, until golden brown and
    cooked through (about 20-25 minutes). Ensure the internal temperature
    reaches 165Â°F (74Â°C).
  4. Rest & Serve: Once cooked, remove the chicken from the pan and let it rest for
    a few minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute, making the
    chicken more tender.

Tips

  • Crispy skin: For extra crispy skin, you can pat the chicken dry with paper towels
    before frying.
  • Frying temperature: Maintain a consistent medium heat to ensure the chicken
    cooks evenly and doesn't burn.
